The vibe

For when Bali feels a bit too obvious.

Taiwan is not trying to be Bali. That’s kind of the point.

Think smaller surf towns, mountain backdrops, noodle shops after a session, scooters down the coast and a bit more “wait, how did no one tell me about this?” energy.

Where to surf

A few Taiwan bases worth knowing.

Dulan

Good first stop for a relaxed surf-town feel. Hostels, lessons, food, bars and an easy east coast vibe.

Taitung / Jinzun

More surf-focused. Better if you care about wave quality, local surf houses and being close to the Taiwan Open area.

Yilan

Closer to Taipei, useful for shorter trips or anyone adding a quick surf mission to a bigger Taiwan itinerary.

Kenting

Southern, warmer, beach-holiday energy. Good if you want surf mixed with a more classic coastal trip.

Quick questions

Is Taiwan actually a surf destination?

Yep. It has east coast surf towns, board rental, surf houses, beginner lessons and a WSL event at Jinzun. It’s just less talked about than the obvious places.

Is this for beginners?

Beginners are welcome. The important thing is choosing the right area, lesson provider and season instead of guessing from old blog posts.

Why not just book a hostel online?

You can. But booking a bed doesn’t tell you whether the surf suits your level, whether you need a scooter, or which local operator is right for the trip you want.
