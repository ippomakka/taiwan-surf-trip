Taiwan surf areas

Where should you actually go?

Start with the area that fits your surf level, season and travel style. Taiwan is small, but picking the wrong base can change the whole trip.

Dulan

Best for surf-town energy

Hostels, lessons, food, bars and an east coast creative vibe. A strong first stop for beginner to intermediate travellers.

Taitung / Jinzun

Best for proper surf focus

More serious surf coastline, local surf houses and the Taiwan Open of Surfing area. Good if waves matter more than nightlife.

Yilan

Best near Taipei

Easier add-on if you’re already flying into Taipei and want lessons, rentals or a shorter surf side quest.

Kenting

Best for southern holiday feel

Warmer, beachy and better for travellers who want surf mixed with a classic coastal holiday atmosphere.

Quick answers

Questions Aussies usually ask first

Is Taiwan actually a surf destination?

Yes. Taiwan has consistent east coast surf, a WSL event at Jinzun, local surf houses and beginner lesson options. It’s just less obvious online than Bali or Sri Lanka.

Is this for beginners or experienced surfers?

Both, but the match matters. Beginners usually need lessons, board rental and safer beach breaks. Experienced surfers may care more about season, local guiding and transport.

Can I do Taiwan as a short trip from Australia?

Possible, but it makes most sense as a 5–10 day Taiwan trip or as part of a broader Asia trip. Singapore and Hong Kong are better for weekend-style testing later.

Why not just book on Hostelworld or Booking.com?

You can book beds there, but they won’t tell you which surf town fits your level, whether you need a scooter, who does lessons, or whether the season suits your trip.
